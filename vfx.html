Shot Breakdown

This is the original green-screen plate alongside the completed final shot.

This sequence began with a green-screen plate of the protagonist, captured with controlled key lighting and practical wind elements to integrate naturally with the intended environment.

  • To build the digital environment, I reconstructed the shot in Blender, matching the live-action camera's focal length, sensor size, and lens characteristics to ensure parallax and spatial continuity between the plate and the CG elements.
  • For the interactive movement in the scene, I generated a cloth simulation on a stand-in model proportioned to the actor. This allowed the simulated fabric to react believably to the same wind direction and intensity present during production.
  • With the 3D renders completed, I transitioned into Adobe After Effects for compositing. There, I tracked the live-action plate, integrated the CG simulation, refined the edges and lighting interactions, and executed the final color treatment to align with the director's stylistic intent.
